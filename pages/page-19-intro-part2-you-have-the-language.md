# Part II Intro: You Have the Language

A landscape-format educational comic book page (2048x1440px). MAP ROOM PAGE — dark background with warm lighting. The second appearance of the journey map, now with Part I checked off and Part II glowing.

The style is polished, modern comic book — clean lines, vibrant colors, professional typography.

IMPORTANT: Do NOT number the panels. No circled numbers or digits in panel corners.

## Characters

**Nityesh:** A pixel-art style young Indian man with distinctive big curly black hair, rectangular glasses, light stubble, white t-shirt, and beaded necklace. Expressive cartoon eyes. The curly hair is his most defining visual feature — big, voluminous, immediately recognizable. Pixel-art aesthetic — visible chunky pixels, retro game feel, but clearly this specific person.

**Helix:** A small, friendly, anthropomorphic double-helix DNA strand. Coral/salmon pink strands with teal/cyan rungs (base pairs) connecting them. Expressive cartoon eyes near the top, small stubby arms and legs. Approachable, curious, slightly nerdy. Standard comic style (NOT pixel-art). About 1/4 of Nityesh's height — a small companion character.

## Goal of This Page

The reader has just finished 16 pages of dense genetics education. This is a BREATHING MOMENT — a chance to feel proud of what they've learned, get grounded, and build excitement for what comes next. The shift from "learning the tools" to "using them on real history" should feel like leveling up.

## Visual Design Notes

- The journey map should be IMMEDIATELY RECOGNIZABLE as the same map from Part I's intro — same layout, same milestones, same style. The only difference: Part I now has a checkmark/gold glow, and Part II is lit up.
- The visual recap icons in slot 4 should be tiny and clean — this isn't re-teaching, it's a quick "remember all this? Good."
- The transition visual in slot 7 (chalkboard → landscape) should feel like a genre shift — from educational to epic. Part II IS more narrative-driven.
- Nityesh's body language should convey growth — he's more confident, more engaged. He earned this.
- The overall emotional tone: accomplishment → anticipation. "Look how much you've learned. Now let's use it."
