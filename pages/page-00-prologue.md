# Prologue: "I Couldn't Follow the Podcast"

A landscape-format educational comic book page (2048x1440px) with a warm, intimate color palette — soft desk lamp lighting, evening tones. Structured 4-column × 2-row grid layout (8 slots). Panels can be merged for emphasis.

The style is polished, modern comic book — clean lines, vibrant colors, professional typography mixed with handwritten-style annotations. Pixel-art elements for Nityesh's character.

IMPORTANT: Do NOT number the panels. No circled numbers or digits in panel corners. The layout flows naturally.

## Characters

**Nityesh:** A pixel-art style young Indian man with distinctive big curly black hair, rectangular glasses, light stubble, white t-shirt, and beaded necklace. Expressive cartoon eyes. The curly hair is his most defining visual feature — big, voluminous, immediately recognizable. Pixel-art aesthetic — visible chunky pixels, retro game feel, but clearly this specific person.

**Helix:** A small, friendly, anthropomorphic double-helix DNA strand. Coral/salmon pink strands with teal/cyan rungs (base pairs) connecting them. Expressive cartoon eyes near the top, small stubby arms and legs. Approachable, curious, slightly nerdy. Standard comic style (NOT pixel-art). About 1/4 of Nityesh's height — a small companion character.

## Goal of This Page

The reader should immediately identify with Nityesh's frustration — wanting to understand something fascinating but feeling locked out by jargon. Then hope arrives: a tutor who speaks your language. The emotional arc is: curiosity → frustration → hope → let's go.

This is the FIRST page of an ~80-page graphic novel. It needs to hook HARD.

## Panel Layout

### Slots 1-2 (top-left, merged 2 slots) — "The Shelf"

A wide establishing shot. Nityesh's desk at evening — warm desk lamp casting golden light. A laptop screen shows the Dwarkesh Podcast player with the episode title visible: "David Reich: How One Small Tribe Conquered the World 70,000 Years Ago." The progress bar shows 0:00 — unplayed. Headphones sit on the desk beside the laptop.

On the desk, scattered around: a couple of books, a coffee mug, phone. The scene feels lived-in and personal. The laptop screen glows as the brightest element.

A caption box at the top in clean typography: "Two things I've always been fascinated by: genetics and ancient history."

A second caption below: "This episode sits right at the intersection. One of Dwarkesh's most popular. Over a million views."

Nityesh sits at the desk, looking at the screen with a mix of longing and hesitation — the expression of someone who's been meaning to do something for months.

### Slot 3 (top-center-right, 1 slot) — "The Problem"

Close-up on Nityesh's face — confused, slightly frustrated. Behind him, speech bubbles float in the air like ghosts, containing jargon from the podcast:

"archaic admixture"
"mitochondrial lineage"
"purifying selection"
"allele frequency"
"gene flow event"

The bubbles are rendered in a cold blue-gray tone, floating and intimidating — words that mean nothing to him yet. They crowd the space around his head.

Caption in handwritten style: "But every time I pressed play, I got lost within minutes."

### Slot 4 (top-right, 1 slot) — "Months on the Shelf"

A small, quiet panel. A visual metaphor: the podcast episode represented as a book or cassette tape sitting on a literal shelf, gathering dust. A small cobweb in the corner. Months have passed.

Caption: "So it sat on the shelf. Months."

The color palette is muted — grays and dusty browns. The feeling is stagnation.

### Slots 5-6 (bottom-left, merged 2 slots) — "Then AI Got Good Enough"

The color temperature SHIFTS — from the dusty grays of slot 4 to warm, vibrant tones. This is the turn.

Nityesh picks up the headphones with a new expression: determination mixed with excitement. The laptop screen now shows a split view — the podcast on one side, and a glowing coral/teal presence forming on the other side of the desk.

Caption in bold: "Then AI got good enough to be my personal tutor."

Below, in handwritten style: "Not a textbook. Not a Wikipedia rabbit hole. An actual tutor who could pause the interview and explain every concept I didn't get."

The warm desk lamp light intensifies — the scene feels alive again.

### Slot 7 (bottom-center-right, 1 slot) — "Enter Helix"

Helix materializes on the desk — a small coral-and-teal DNA strand character, blinking into existence with a subtle glow. Helix looks up at Nityesh with a friendly wave and a warm smile. Small sparkle effects around Helix suggest they've just appeared.

Helix's speech bubble: "Hey. I'm Helix. Before we press play, let me teach you my language."

Nityesh looks down at Helix with surprised delight — the expression of someone who just found exactly what they needed.

### Slot 8 (bottom-right, 1 slot) — "Let's Go"

A punchy closer. Nityesh puts on the headphones. Helix sits on the desk next to the laptop, looking at the reader with a confident, inviting expression.

Helix: "Then you'll understand everything he says."

Bold text at the bottom: "PART I — THE LANGUAGE OF LIFE"

The background shifts to a clean warm cream — a visual bridge to the teaching pages that follow. The feeling is: the journey begins NOW.

## Visual Design Notes

- The page literally gets WARMER as you read it: dusty/cool in the top-right (frustration, shelf) → warm golden tones in the bottom-left (hope, determination) → vibrant coral/teal in the bottom-right (Helix arrives)
- Nityesh is pixel-art style throughout — chunky, retro game aesthetic, but clearly recognizable
- Helix is standard comic style — smooth lines, not pixelated. The visual contrast between the two characters is intentional and should be maintained across all pages
- The desk lamp is the primary light source — everything is lit from its warm glow
- The jargon speech bubbles in slot 3 should feel ALIEN and intimidating — cold colors, slightly distorted text, floating ominously
- The podcast player on the laptop should look realistic — recognizable as a podcast interface
- This is page 1 of ~80 pages. It needs to feel like the opening of something epic — a personal journey that's about to begin
