# Chapter 4, Page 16: The Cast Assembles

A landscape-format educational comic book page (2048x1440px). INTERVIEW PAGE — clean white/cool background with a GRAND, panoramic feel. This is the summary page of Part I — a visual family portrait of all known human species, the braided river model, and the setup for Part II.

The style is polished, modern comic book — clean lines, vibrant colors, professional typography.

IMPORTANT: Do NOT number the panels. No circled numbers or digits in panel corners.

## Characters

**Nityesh:** A pixel-art style young Indian man with distinctive big curly black hair, rectangular glasses, light stubble, white t-shirt, and beaded necklace. Expressive cartoon eyes. The curly hair is his most defining visual feature — big, voluminous, immediately recognizable. Pixel-art aesthetic — visible chunky pixels, retro game feel, but clearly this specific person.

**Helix:** A small, friendly, anthropomorphic double-helix DNA strand. Coral/salmon pink strands with teal/cyan rungs (base pairs) connecting them. Expressive cartoon eyes near the top, small stubby arms and legs. Approachable, curious, slightly nerdy. Standard comic style (NOT pixel-art). About 1/4 of Nityesh's height — a small companion character.

**David Reich:** Head and shoulders only (no full body). Middle-aged, short brown receding hair, blue collared shirt. Warm, enthusiastic academic energy. Shares findings with genuine excitement. Standard comic style.

## Goal of This Page

Present the complete cast of human species we now know existed, show WHEN they lived and how they're related, establish the braided river as the correct model (not the tree), and transition into Part II (The Deep Past). The reader should feel the full scope of human diversity across time — and the realization that most of this was unknown before ancient DNA.

## Panel Layout

### Slots 1-4 (ENTIRE TOP ROW merged) — "The Family Portrait"

A MASSIVE panoramic panel spanning the full width.

A timeline spanning from 2 million years ago (left) to present day (right). Along this timeline, the known human species are shown as colored bands — each band representing when a species existed, with their geographic range suggested by vertical position:

**The Species (top to bottom, roughly by geography):**

- **Homo erectus** — the globe-trotter. Long band from ~1.9 million to ~100,000 years ago. Widest geographic spread. Shown as a sturdy silhouette. Label: "The original world traveler."

- **Superarchaic humans** — a ghostly, semi-transparent band. Split ~1 million years ago. Dotted outline (ghost population). Label: "Zero bones. Only math."

- **Denisovans** — a semi-transparent amber band. Eastern Eurasia. Known only from DNA + a few fragments. Label: "A pinky bone and giant teeth."

- **Neanderthals** — a solid band in cool blue/gray. Western Eurasia, ~400,000-40,000 years ago. Label: "Our closest known relatives."

- **Homo sapiens** — warm band originating in Africa ~300,000 years ago, then expanding globally after ~70,000 years ago.

- **Unknown African archaic lineages** — ghostly bands within Africa. Dotted outlines. Label: "Inferred from modern African DNA."

- **Homo floresiensis** — tiny band on Flores island, Indonesia. Label: "The 'hobbits.'"

- **Homo naledi** — band in South Africa, puzzlingly recent. Label: "Found in caves. Puzzlingly recent."

**ARROWS** between the bands show gene flow / admixture events — where species MET and MIXED. Key arrows:
- Modern humans ↔ Neanderthals (~54,000-49,000 years ago)
- Modern humans ↔ Denisovans
- Denisovans ↔ Superarchaics
- Unknown flows within Africa

The visual should NOT look like a clean family tree. It should look like a BRAIDED RIVER — bands splitting, merging, some drying up. The arrows of gene flow are the braids.

A podcast-style speech bubble from Reich: "The model that we have is really a model based on accretion... one wonders whether there's some pretty fundamental differences that might explain the patterns."

### Slots 5-6 (bottom-left, merged 2 slots) — "Not a Tree — A River"

The key visual comparison, side by side:

**LEFT — THE OLD MODEL:** A clean, branching family tree. One trunk splits into neat branches. Modern humans on top, Neanderthals on a side branch, everything orderly. A RED X through it.

**RIGHT — THE NEW MODEL:** The BRAIDED RIVER — a gorgeous landscape illustration showing multiple streams flowing across a continental map over time. Streams split and merge, some dry up, new ones form, none are ever completely separate. Color-coded to match the species bands from the top panel.

"This is NOT a family tree. It's a braided river — lineages splitting, merging, some dying out, new ones forming, never completely separate."

The braided river should be the MOST BEAUTIFUL single image on the page — it's the visual thesis of the entire book.

### Slot 7 (bottom-center-right, 1 slot) — "What We Didn't Know"

A TIMELINE OF DISCOVERIES — showing how fast the field moved:

A cascading visual, year by year:
- **Pre-2010:** 3 known species. Clean tree. Simple.
- **2010:** Denisovans discovered from a pinky bone
- **2012:** Superarchaic ghost population detected in Denisovan genome
- **2014:** Tibetan altitude gene traced to Denisovans
- **2015:** Petrous bone revolution — success rates skyrocket
- **2016:** African ghost populations inferred
- **2018:** First Denisovan jaw found outside Denisova Cave

Each year ADDS a new species, connection, or discovery to the growing braided river. The visual point: ALL of this happened in 15 years. The pace of discovery is accelerating.

Helix: "Every time we look somewhere new, we find something we didn't expect. And we've barely started looking."

## Visual Design Notes

- The top-row panoramic timeline should be POSTER-QUALITY — this is a reference image the reader will mentally return to throughout the book. It should be dense with information but never cluttered. Each species band should be immediately distinguishable.
- Ghost populations (superarchaics, unnamed African archaics) should be visually distinct from confirmed species — translucent, dotted borders, question marks. They're real but mysterious.
- The braided river in slots 5-6 should be the single most beautiful illustration in the chapter — a landscape, not a diagram. Flowing water, warm and cool tones merging, terrain that suggests real geography.
- The before/after comparison in slot 7 delivers the emotional point: ancient DNA didn't just add detail to a known story — it REPLACED the story entirely.
- The transition to Part II should feel like the moment in a movie when the training montage ends and the real adventure begins. "The encounter that changed YOU" personalizes the stakes.
- Color coding for species should be established here and maintained throughout the book: Neanderthals = cool blue/gray, Denisovans = amber, Superarchaics = dark ghostly, Modern humans = warm.
