# Chapter 3, Page 12: When the Stories Disagree

A landscape-format educational comic book page (2048x1440px). This is an INTERVIEW PAGE — clean white/cool background, the podcast is playing, David Reich speaks for the first time as a visual character. This is the climax of Part I's setup — the mystery that drives the entire podcast.

The style is polished, modern comic book — clean lines, vibrant colors, professional typography. More structured grid than the teaching pages.

IMPORTANT: Do NOT number the panels. No circled numbers or digits in panel corners.

## Characters

**Nityesh:** A pixel-art style young Indian man with distinctive big curly black hair, rectangular glasses, light stubble, white t-shirt, and beaded necklace. Expressive cartoon eyes. The curly hair is his most defining visual feature — big, voluminous, immediately recognizable. Pixel-art aesthetic — visible chunky pixels, retro game feel, but clearly this specific person.

**Helix:** A small, friendly, anthropomorphic double-helix DNA strand. Coral/salmon pink strands with teal/cyan rungs (base pairs) connecting them. Expressive cartoon eyes near the top, small stubby arms and legs. Approachable, curious, slightly nerdy. Standard comic style (NOT pixel-art). About 1/4 of Nityesh's height — a small companion character.

**David Reich:** Head and shoulders only (no full body). Middle-aged, short brown receding hair, blue collared shirt. Warm, enthusiastic academic energy. Shares findings with genuine excitement. Standard comic style.

## Goal of This Page

This is the PAYOFF page. Present the discordance between nuclear DNA, mtDNA, and Y chromosome divergence dates for humans vs Neanderthals. Explain what it means (gene flow into Neanderthals). Introduce the Ptolemy epicycles metaphor. Build to Reich's radical conclusion: maybe the line between "modern" and "archaic" humans dissolves. The reader should finish this page with their mental model shattered and hungry for the deep history that follows.

## Panel Layout

### Slots 1-2 (top-left, merged 2 slots) — "The Discordance"

Wide panel. Clean white background with cool blue accents — INTERVIEW style.

Three horizontal timelines, large and prominent, stacked vertically:

**Timeline 1 — NUCLEAR DNA (gray/neutral):**
A bar stretching from 0 to 750,000 years. The human-Neanderthal split marked at **500,000-750,000 years ago**. Label: "Nuclear DNA says: split ~500,000-750,000 years ago."

**Timeline 2 — mtDNA (coral/pink):**
A shorter bar. Split marked at **~400,000-500,000 years ago**. Label: "mtDNA says: split ~400,000-500,000 years ago."

**Timeline 3 — Y CHROMOSOME (blue):**
Split marked at **~300,000-400,000 years ago**. Label: "Y chromosome says: split ~300,000-400,000 years ago."

**Timeline 4 — DENISOVANS (amber, for comparison):**
A fourth bar showing Denisovan mtDNA and Y divergence at **700,000-1,000,000 years ago** — MUCH deeper, consistent with or exceeding the nuclear estimate. Label: "Denisovans: mtDNA and Y show 700k-1M years. The pattern is specific to Neanderthals."

The GAP between the nuclear date and the uniparental dates for Neanderthals is highlighted with a large RED QUESTION MARK and red shading. The Denisovan bar shows NO such gap — ruling out a simple clock-calibration error.

Bold text below: "Both uniparental markers show MUCH younger splits than nuclear DNA for Neanderthals — but NOT for Denisovans. Something specific happened to the Neanderthal maternal and paternal lineages."

A podcast-style speech bubble (with waveform edges) from Dwarkesh's voice: "What does that discrepancy tell us?"

### Slot 3 (top-center-right, 1 slot) — "The Replacement"

David Reich appears as a VISUAL CHARACTER for the first time — head and shoulders, speaking with animated enthusiasm, gesturing with one hand.

Reich's speech bubble: "The most likely explanation is gene flow FROM modern humans INTO Neanderthals — specifically replacing their mtDNA and Y chromosome lineages."

Helix, standing on Nityesh's desk nearby, adds context: "Imagine a population of Neanderthals that keeps receiving waves of modern human migrants. Over time, the migrants' maternal and paternal lineages replace the originals — even though most of the nuclear DNA stays Neanderthal."

A small probability callout: "In a population of typical Neanderthal size (~1,000-10,000), the probability of BOTH mtDNA and Y being replaced by random drift alone: ~5% × 5% = 0.25%"

### Slot 4 (top-right, 1 slot) — "Ptolemy's Epicycles"

A beautiful visual metaphor from the podcast:

A family tree diagram (the "standard model" of human evolution) covered in increasingly absurd post-it notes, patches, and duct tape — "exception for Neanderthal mtDNA," "special case for Y chromosome," "add epicycle for Denisovan admixture," "patch for superarchaic signal." The tree is COLLAPSING under its own corrections, branches sagging, held together with tape.

Below: the braided river (previewing slot 7) — elegant, simple, no patches needed.

Reich's voice: "Scientists keep adding epicycles — patches to make the standard model work. Maybe the whole model needs rethinking."

The visual makes the Ptolemy metaphor CONCRETE — the patched tree IS the epicycles. The braided river IS the heliocentric model.

### Slots 5-6 (bottom-left, merged 2 slots) — "The Radical Alternative"

A wide, dramatic panel. The emotional climax of the chapter.

Reich, speaking with intensity — this is the moment the interview gets explosive:

"Maybe it's not 3-8% modern human DNA in Neanderthals but 30%, 50%, even 70%."

"In that case, maybe it's not clear what's modern and what's archaic."

"Are modern humans archaic? Are Neanderthals modern?"

The visual: TWO figures — a modern human silhouette and a Neanderthal silhouette — shown first as clearly separate (the old model), then BLENDING into each other, the boundary dissolving. The line between them becomes a gradient, not a wall.

"Modern humans and Neanderthals are just as qualified to be sisters as Neanderthals and Denisovans."

Nityesh, stunned: "So the whole classification might be wrong?"

### Slot 7 (bottom-center-right, 1 slot) — "The Braided River"

Helix delivers the reframe:

"Think of it less like a family tree with clean branches..."

A small image of a traditional family tree — clean, branching, orderly — with a RED X through it.

"...and more like a braided river."

Below: a gorgeous illustration of a braided river — multiple streams splitting and merging, flowing across a landscape, never fully separate, never fully merged. The streams are color-coded: warm tones for one lineage, cool tones for another, merging where they meet.

"Streams splitting and merging, over and over. No pure populations. No clean boundaries. Just continuous mixing across hundreds of thousands of years."

This is THE visual metaphor for the entire book — established here, referenced in every subsequent chapter.

### Slot 8 (bottom-right, 1 slot) — Hook Panel

Nityesh takes off his headphones. The podcast player shows maybe 15 minutes in. His expression: the world just shifted.

"But how do we even KNOW what Neanderthal DNA looks like? How do you read DNA from a 50,000-year-old bone?"

Helix: "That's the technology that made everything we just heard possible. And the story of how it was built is almost as wild as what it found."

Bold text at bottom: "Chapter 4: Reading the Dead"

## Visual Design Notes

- This is the FIRST full INTERVIEW PAGE — clean white/cool background, structured grid, podcast elements. Visually distinct from all the warm cream teaching pages before it. The reader should FEEL the shift.
- David Reich's VISUAL DEBUT in slot 3 should be striking — this is the first time we see the man whose ideas drive the book. Make him warm and enthusiastic, not stern.
- The three timelines in slots 1-2 should be the most visually prominent element on the page — large, clear, impossible to miss. The red question mark in the gap is the visual thesis.
- The Ptolemy epicycles in slot 4 should be beautiful — the baroque complexity of the wrong model vs the elegant simplicity of the right one. This metaphor recurs in the book.
- The dissolving boundary between modern human and Neanderthal silhouettes in slots 5-6 is the emotional centerpiece — the visual equivalent of Reich's radical idea.
- The braided river in slot 7 is THE defining image of the book. It should be gorgeous — not a diagram but a LANDSCAPE. Warm and cool streams merging across terrain. This image sets the visual language for everything that follows.
- The hook into Chapter 4 shifts from the conceptual ("what does it mean?") to the practical ("how do you actually DO this?"). The reader needs a breath after the conceptual earthquake of this page.
