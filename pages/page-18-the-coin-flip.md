## Goal of This Page

Genetic drift and the founder effect. TWO concepts: (1) Genetic drift — random changes in allele frequency that are powerful in small populations (coin flip analogy), and (2) The founder effect — a small group splitting off carries only a subset of diversity, and that random subset defines billions of descendants.

## Panel Layout

### Slots 1-2 (top-left, merged 2 slots) — "The Jar Experiment"

Wide panel. Genetic drift shown as colored balls in glass jars — a visual sequence:

**Jar 1 (Generation 1):** 50% red balls, 50% blue balls. A large jar, well-mixed.

**Jar 2 (Generation 2):** Someone pours HALF the balls into a new jar. By chance: 60% red, 40% blue. Label: "Random sampling"

**Jar 3 (Generation 3):** Pour half again. Now: 75% red, 25% blue.

**Jar 4 (Generation 4):** 90% red, 10% blue. The blue is almost gone.

**Jar 5 (Generation 5):** 100% red. Blue is EXTINCT. Not because red was "better" — pure chance.

Helix: "In a small population, alleles can disappear entirely by pure chance. No selection involved. Just the luck of who reproduces."

Handwritten: "Flip a coin 10 times → might get 7 heads. Flip 10,000 times → very close to 50/50. Small populations = more randomness."

### Slot 3 (top-center-right, 1 slot) — "The Founder Effect"

A visual: a large jar of mixed balls (the original population). A small scoop takes out a handful — the handful has a different ratio than the original jar. That handful IS the founding population.

Label on scoop: "~250 people leave Africa"
Label on handful: "Their random subset of alleles now defines BILLIONS of descendants"

### Slot 4 (top-right, 1 slot) — Nityesh Absorbs

Nityesh, stunned by the implications.

Speech bubble: "So a tiny group that leaves Africa might just HAPPEN to lose certain alleles? Not because those alleles were bad — just because... luck?"

### Slots 5-6 (bottom-left, merged 2 slots) — "The Biggest Lottery"

Helix, serious, driving the point home.

Speech bubble: "The Out of Africa migration. Maybe 250 people. Their random subset of genes — whatever they happened to carry — now defines billions of non-African descendants. The biggest genetic lottery in human history."

Visual: A small group of human silhouettes leaving through a narrow passage (representing the migration out of Africa). Behind them, a vast diverse population. The narrow passage visually constrains the genetic diversity — a bottleneck.

Handwritten: "Bottleneck: a population crash that randomly eliminates alleles. The survivors' genes dominate all future generations."

### Slot 7 (bottom-center-right, 1 slot) — Nityesh Connects

Nityesh: "And that's why non-Africans have LESS genetic diversity than Africans? Because we all descend from that tiny group's random subset?"

Helix: "Exactly. Africa has more human genetic diversity than the rest of the world combined. Because they kept the full jar. Everyone else got a scoop."

### Slot 8 (bottom-right, 1 slot) — The Hook

Nityesh, processing. Then a new question:

"Okay — so mutations create variety, selection shapes what survives, and drift is the wildcard. But there's one more thing. What happens when two separate populations MEET again? When their genes... mix?"

Helix: "Gene flow. The river between populations. And it's what David Reich studies every single day."
