# Chapter 3, Page 1: Typos in the Code

A landscape-format educational comic book page (2048x1440px). This is a HELIX TEACHING PAGE — warm cream/chalkboard background tone, freeform layout that feels like a whiteboard session.

The style is polished, modern comic book — clean lines, vibrant colors, professional typography mixed with handwritten-style annotations.

IMPORTANT: Do NOT number the panels. No circled numbers or digits in panel corners.

## Characters

**Nityesh:** A pixel-art style young Indian man with distinctive big curly black hair, rectangular glasses, light stubble, white t-shirt, and beaded necklace. Expressive cartoon eyes. The curly hair is his most defining visual feature — big, voluminous, immediately recognizable. Pixel-art aesthetic — visible chunky pixels, retro game feel, but clearly this specific person.

**Helix:** A small, friendly, anthropomorphic double-helix DNA strand. Coral/salmon pink strands with teal/cyan rungs (base pairs) connecting them. Expressive cartoon eyes near the top, small stubby arms and legs. Approachable, curious, slightly nerdy. Standard comic style (NOT pixel-art). About 1/4 of Nityesh's height — a small companion character.

## Goal of This Page

Introduce mutations using the sentence analogy. Just TWO concepts: (1) Mutations are permanent changes in the DNA sequence — typos during copying, and (2) Three types of point mutations shown through the sentence analogy: silent (no effect), missense (changed meaning), nonsense (premature stop). That's it — frameshifts, structural mutations, and mutation rate are on the NEXT page.

## Panel Layout

### Slots 1-2 (top-left, merged 2 slots) — "The Typo"

Wide panel. Helix stands at a chalkboard showing a sentence:

**Original:** "THE BIG RED FOX"

Helix: "When a cell copies its 3.2 billion letters of DNA, sometimes it makes a mistake. One wrong letter. A MUTATION."

Below the original sentence, three variations:

### Slot 3 (top-center-right, 1 slot) — "Silent"

"THE BIG RED FO**Z**" — but the word still means the same thing (illustrated: the fox is still clearly a fox).

Label: "SILENT MUTATION — Different letter, same amino acid. No effect."

Handwritten: "The genetic code has redundancy — multiple codons can code for the same amino acid. So some typos are invisible."

### Slot 4 (top-right, 1 slot) — "Missense"

"THE BIG RED **B**OX" — one letter changed, completely different meaning. Illustrated: a box appears where the fox was.

Label: "MISSENSE — Different letter, different amino acid. Changed protein."

Handwritten: "Can be harmful, neutral, or occasionally beneficial."

### Slots 5-6 (bottom-left, merged 2 slots) — "Nonsense"

"THE BIG RED **.**" — a period (stop codon) appears mid-sentence. Everything after is gone. The rest of the sentence fades into nothing.

Label: "NONSENSE — A premature stop signal. The protein is cut short."

Helix: "Imagine a recipe that stops halfway through. You get half a cake. Usually useless."

Nityesh, looking at the three examples: "So mutations are just typos. Some are harmless, some change the meaning, and some stop the sentence cold."

### Slot 7 (bottom-center-right, 1 slot) — Nityesh Processes

A quiet beat. Nityesh looks at his own hands — thinking about mutations happening inside him right now.

Speech bubble: "And these are happening inside me? Right now?"

Helix: "Your cells are copying DNA as we speak. Most errors get caught by proofreading enzymes. But a few slip through every generation."

### Slot 8 (bottom-right, 1 slot) — The Hook

Nityesh: "How many slip through? And what happens when the typo is BIGGER than one letter?"

Helix: "Good questions. Both."
