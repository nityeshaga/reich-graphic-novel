# Chapter 1, Page 1: Meet the Molecule

A landscape-format educational comic book page (2048x1440px). This is a HELIX TEACHING PAGE — warm cream/chalkboard background tone, freeform layout that feels like a whiteboard session. Less rigid than the interview pages. The podcast is paused. We're in "tutor mode."

The style is polished, modern comic book — clean lines, vibrant colors, professional typography mixed with handwritten-style annotations. Diagrams should feel like beautiful science illustrations, not textbook drudgery.

IMPORTANT: Do NOT number the panels. No circled numbers or digits in panel corners.

## Characters

**Nityesh:** A pixel-art style young Indian man with distinctive big curly black hair, rectangular glasses, light stubble, white t-shirt, and beaded necklace. Expressive cartoon eyes. The curly hair is his most defining visual feature — big, voluminous, immediately recognizable. Pixel-art aesthetic — visible chunky pixels, retro game feel, but clearly this specific person.

**Helix:** A small, friendly, anthropomorphic double-helix DNA strand. Coral/salmon pink strands with teal/cyan rungs (base pairs) connecting them. Expressive cartoon eyes near the top, small stubby arms and legs. Approachable, curious, slightly nerdy. Standard comic style (NOT pixel-art). About 1/4 of Nityesh's height — a small companion character. On this page, Helix is the TEACHER — confident, animated, gesturing at diagrams.

## Goal of This Page

Introduce DNA structure — the double helix and base pairing rules. Just TWO concepts: (1) DNA is a twisted ladder of two strands, and (2) four letters pair up in fixed rules (A-T, G-C). The reader should finish this page knowing what DNA physically looks like and that it's built from a simple alphabet.

This is the FIRST teaching page. It needs to feel inviting, not overwhelming. Helix is an enthusiastic tutor, not a textbook.

## Panel Layout

### Slots 1-2 (top-left, merged 2 slots) — "I Am the Molecule"

Wide panel. Helix stands center-stage, arms spread wide, presenting themselves as a living exhibit. Behind Helix, a LARGE, beautiful scientific illustration of the DNA double helix — the iconic twisted ladder structure, rendered gorgeously with soft glowing lines. The illustration is semi-transparent and fills the background.

Clean annotation lines label key parts:
- **Sugar-phosphate backbone** — the two "rails" of the ladder, labeled with an arrow
- **Base pairs** — the "rungs," showing A-T and G-C pairings
- **The twist** — one full helical turn visible
- **3' and 5' ends** — small labels showing the strands run in opposite directions (antiparallel)

Helix's speech bubble: "I'm not just a mascot. I'm literally what I look like — two strands, wound around each other, held together by base pairs."

Nityesh sits in the lower corner, leaning forward, curious and engaged.

### Slot 3 (top-right, 1 slot) — "The Four Letters"

A clean, colorful diagram showing the four nucleotide bases, each with its own color:

- **Adenine (A)** — green, labeled "pairs with T"
- **Thymine (T)** — red, labeled "pairs with A"
- **Guanine (G)** — blue, labeled "pairs with C"
- **Cytosine (C)** — yellow, labeled "pairs with G"

Shown as two pairs with connections:
A ←→ T (two hydrogen bonds, shown as two dashes)
G ←→ C (three hydrogen bonds, shown as three dashes)

Handwritten annotation: "Purines (A, G) are big. Pyrimidines (T, C) are small. A big one always pairs with a small one — that's what keeps the width constant."

### Slot 4 (top-right, 1 slot) — Nityesh Reacts

Close-up on Nityesh's face — eyebrows raised, genuine surprise.

Speech bubble: "Wait, that's IT? Four letters?"

### Slot 5 (bottom-left, 1 slot) — Helix Answers

Helix, perched on the edge of the DNA diagram, looking down at Nityesh with a knowing grin.

Speech bubble: "Four letters. But those four letters write the instructions for every living thing on Earth."

### Slots 6-7 (bottom-center, merged 2 slots) — "Unzipping"

Helix literally unzips themselves — the two strands of their body separating to reveal the base pairs between them. A visual gag that doubles as education. The separated strands show the complementary bases facing each other: A matched with T, G matched with C. Color-coded to match the diagram above.

Handwritten annotation along the separated strands: "Know one strand, you know the other. They're complementary."

Small speech bubble from Helix (wincing slightly at being unzipped): "This is what I look like on the inside. Literally."

### Slot 8 (bottom-right, 1 slot) — The Hook

Nityesh looks at his own hands, then at a small potted plant on the desk beside him. Thoughtful expression.

Speech bubble: "Every living thing? All from four letters? A plant and me... use the same alphabet?"

Helix, hopping back together (re-zipping), beckons Nityesh to follow: "Come on. Let me show you HOW MUCH of it you carry."
