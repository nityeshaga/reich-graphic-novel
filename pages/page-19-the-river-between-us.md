The curly hair is his most defining visual feature — big, voluminous, immediately recognizable. Pixel-art aesthetic — visible chunky pixels, retro game feel, but clearly this specific person.

## Goal of This Page

Gene flow and the synthesis of all forces. TWO concepts: (1) Gene flow — when populations interbreed, they exchange alleles, and even one migrant per generation can prevent complete divergence, and (2) the four forces together (mutation, selection, drift, gene flow) are what change allele frequencies, and measuring those changes is what Reich does. This page closes Chapter 3 and bridges naturally to Chapter 4 (how DNA copies and shuffles).

## Panel Layout

### Slots 1-2 (top-left, merged 2 slots) — "Gene Flow"

Wide panel. Two population circles connected by a narrow bridge. A few individuals (small figures) cross the bridge in both directions. The allele frequency dots in each circle are becoming more similar where the bridge connects them.

Helix: "Gene flow. When individuals from one population breed with another, they carry their alleles with them. Even a trickle — one migrant per generation — can keep two populations genetically connected."

Handwritten: "Gene flow is a river between populations. It prevents complete divergence."

### Slot 3 (top-center-right, 1 slot) — "When Flow Stops"

The bridge is broken. The two circles are now isolated. Their dot patterns are diverging rapidly.

Helix: "Cut the flow? The populations drift apart. Different mutations accumulate. Different selection pressures act. Given enough time, they become genetically distinct."

### Slot 4 (top-right, 1 slot) — Nityesh Connects

Nityesh: "So when Reich finds gene flow between modern humans and Neanderthals, he's saying the bridge was open? They were interbreeding?"

Helix: "And when he can MEASURE how much flow occurred, he can tell you what percentage of your ancestry traces to that bridge."

### Slots 5-6 (bottom-left, merged 2 slots) — "The Four Forces"

A synthesis panel — beautiful, balanced. A compass-like diagram with four forces pulling in different directions, with ALLELE FREQUENCY at the center:

- **Mutation** (pointing up): "Creates new variants"
- **Selection** (pointing right): "Favors some, removes others"
- **Drift** (pointing down): "Random change, strongest in small populations"
- **Gene Flow** (pointing left): "Connects populations, shares variants"

Helix: "Four forces. They push allele frequencies around. Measuring HOW they changed, and WHEN, is the entire game of population genetics."

### Slot 7 (bottom-center-right, 1 slot) — Nityesh's Synthesis

The big moment — Nityesh putting it all together. His expression is one of quiet confidence, not excitement. He UNDERSTANDS now.

Speech bubble: "So mutations create variety. Selection and drift shape which variants survive. Gene flow moves variants between populations. And counting the differences between genomes tells you WHEN populations diverged. That's... that's the whole framework."

Helix: "You just described the entire field of population genetics. In four sentences."

### Slot 8 (bottom-right, 1 slot) — The Bridge to Chapter 4

Nityesh, having just synthesized the big picture, now notices a gap.

Speech bubble: "But all of this depends on DNA being COPIED — from parent to child, generation after generation. You showed me replication back in Chapter 1, but... how does the DNA get from my parents into ME? Not the copying — the DELIVERY system."

Helix: "Ah. Now you're asking about cell division. And there are TWO kinds — one is boring, and the other is the most important biological process for everything David Reich does."

Nityesh: "Show me the important one."

Helix: "I'll show you both. You need to see the boring one first to understand why the other one is magic."
