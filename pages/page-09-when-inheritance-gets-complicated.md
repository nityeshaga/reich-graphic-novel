## Goal of This Page

Show that inheritance isn't always dominant/recessive. TWO concepts: (1) Codominance — both alleles fully expressed (blood type AB), and (2) Incomplete dominance — blending (red + white = pink flowers). The reader should understand that Mendel's clean rules were the starting point, not the whole story.

## Panel Layout

### Slots 1-2 (top-left, merged 2 slots) — "Not Always a Boss Fight"

Wide panel. Helix stands between two scenarios, arms spread, presenting both:

Helix: "Mendel's peas were clean — one allele dominates, the other hides. But nature isn't always that simple. Sometimes alleles SHARE power."

Left side of the panel is labeled "CODOMINANCE" and right side "INCOMPLETE DOMINANCE" with a dividing line between them.

### Slot 3 (top-center-right, 1 slot) — "Codominance: Blood Type"

A vivid diagram of blood type inheritance. Three red blood cells shown:
- Type A cell: has A-shaped sugar markers on surface, colored red
- Type B cell: has B-shaped sugar markers, colored blue  
- Type AB cell: has BOTH A and B markers, colored purple/mixed

Helix: "Blood type. A and B are codominant — if you have one of each, you don't get a blend. You get BOTH. Type AB has both markers sitting right there on your red blood cells."

Handwritten: "Neither allele masks the other. Both fully expressed."

### Slot 4 (top-right, 1 slot) — "Incomplete Dominance: The Blend"

A beautiful visual: a red snapdragon flower + a white snapdragon flower → a PINK offspring flower.

Nityesh mixes red and white paint on a palette as a visual metaphor — the colors blend.

Handwritten: "Red × White = Pink. The heterozygote looks DIFFERENT from both homozygotes."

### Slot 5 (bottom-left, 1 slot) — Nityesh Sums Up

Nityesh counting on his fingers, organizing the three patterns:

Speech bubble: "Okay, so sometimes one allele wins completely — dominant. Sometimes they both show up at full strength — codominant. And sometimes they actually blend — incomplete dominance."

### Slot 6 (bottom-center-left, 1 slot) — Helix Confirms

Helix nods approvingly.

Speech bubble: "Three different power-sharing arrangements. But here's the thing — all of these examples are still SINGLE genes controlling a trait. One gene, two alleles, clear outcome."

### Slot 7 (bottom-center-right, 1 slot) — The Pivot

Helix's expression shifts to something more serious and excited — the "here comes the big idea" look.

Speech bubble: "But skin color isn't controlled by just one gene, right? It's not like there's a 'light' allele and a 'dark' allele with one Punnett square."

### Slot 8 (bottom-right, 1 slot) — The Hook

Nityesh, looking at his own arm — his skin tone, clearly a complex trait.

Nityesh: "Yeah... obviously not. So what IS controlling it?"

Helix pulls up a visualization — hundreds of tiny dimmer switches, all contributing to one trait. A preview of the next page.

Helix: "Not one gene. A SYMPHONY."
