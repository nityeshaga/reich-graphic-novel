## Goal of This Page

Two concepts: (1) proteins do everything in your body — enzymes, structural proteins, transport, immune defense, hormones — and (2) only ~1.5% of your genome actually codes for proteins (the "junk DNA" surprise). This page lands the Central Dogma arc and bridges to the next chapter on inheritance.

This is the LAST page of Chapter 1. The hook must naturally raise the question of inheritance — why do you look like your parents? — which launches Chapter 2.

## Panel Layout

### Slots 1-2 (top-left, merged 2 slots) — "The Protein Shake Moment"

Wide panel. The moment of realization. Nityesh holds his protein shake, staring at it. A thought bubble traces the full chain backward:

**Protein shake** ← amino acids ← assembled by ribosomes ← reading mRNA ← copied from DNA

The chain is drawn as a literal visual trail — each step connected by arrows, floating above Nityesh's head like a thought experiment. Each step is illustrated with small icons from the previous pages (the ribosome, the mRNA tape, the vault).

Nityesh: "The protein in my shake was made from amino acids... which were assembled by ribosomes... reading instructions copied from DNA. It's the same chain. In me, in the cow, in the whey."

Helix, sitting on the shake bottle: "Now you're seeing it."

### Slots 3-4 (top-right, merged 2 slots) — "What Proteins Do"

A montage panel — a grid of small, vivid vignettes showing different proteins in action, each with a one-line label:

- **Hemoglobin** carrying an oxygen molecule through a red blood vessel (label: "TRANSPORT — hemoglobin carries oxygen")
- **Collagen** fibers holding skin cells together like scaffolding (label: "STRUCTURE — collagen holds you together")
- **An antibody** (Y-shaped) tackling a virus (label: "DEFENSE — antibodies fight invaders")
- **Insulin** (shown as a key) unlocking a cell to let sugar in (label: "HORMONES — insulin opens the door for glucose")
- **An enzyme** (shown as scissors) snipping a molecule in half (label: "ENZYMES — speed up chemical reactions")

Below the montage, a summary line in Helix's handwriting: "A gene is ultimately an instruction to build a specific protein. Proteins build and run everything."

### Slot 5 (bottom-left, 1 slot) — "The 1.5% Surprise"

A pie chart — but dramatic. A HUGE gray circle (98.5%) with a tiny, brightly glowing sliver (1.5%).

Label on the sliver: "Genes that code for proteins: 1.5%"
Label on the gray: "The rest: once called 'junk DNA'"

Handwritten annotation: "Now we know much of the 'junk' has regulatory functions — turning genes on and off in different tissues. Not junk at all."

Helix, standing next to the pie chart: "Your genome is 3.2 billion letters. Only about 1.5% of that builds proteins. The rest controls WHEN and WHERE those proteins get built."

### Slot 6 (bottom-center-left, 1 slot) — Nityesh Sums It Up

Nityesh, looking up from the shake bottle, ticking off on his fingers. Expression: satisfied, like he's locking something in.

Speech bubble: "So DNA makes RNA, RNA makes proteins, and proteins build... everything. My muscles, my immune system, my hair, the hemoglobin in my blood."
