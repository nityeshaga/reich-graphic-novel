## Characters

**Nityesh:** A pixel-art style young Indian man with distinctive big curly black hair, rectangular glasses, light stubble, white t-shirt, and beaded necklace. Expressive cartoon eyes. The curly hair is his most defining visual feature — big, voluminous, immediately recognizable. Pixel-art aesthetic — visible chunky pixels, retro game feel, but clearly this specific person.

**Helix:** A small, friendly, anthropomorphic double-helix DNA strand. Coral/salmon pink strands with teal/cyan rungs (base pairs) connecting them. Expressive cartoon eyes near the top, small stubby arms and legs. Approachable, curious, slightly nerdy. Standard comic style (NOT pixel-art). About 1/4 of Nityesh's height — a small companion character.

## Goal of This Page

Blow Nityesh's mind with SCALE. Two concepts: (1) Your genome is 3.2 billion base pairs — if uncoiled, 2 meters per cell, and (2) DNA is packaged into 23 pairs of chromosomes. The reader should feel awe at the sheer amount of information crammed inside every cell of their body.

This page is about wonder, not memorization. The zoom sequence is the visual centerpiece.

## Panel Layout

### Slots 1-4 (entire top row, merged) — "The Zoom"

A wide cinematic panel showing a ZOOM SEQUENCE — a continuous visual that flows left to right across the entire top row, each stage getting more detailed:

**Stage 1 (far left):** Nityesh's full body, standing. A small glowing circle highlights a patch of skin on his arm. Label: "Your body: ~37 trillion cells"

**Stage 2:** Zoom into the skin — individual cells visible, each with a nucleus glowing faintly. Label: "Every cell has a nucleus"

**Stage 3:** Zoom into the nucleus — dark sphere packed with tangled structures. Chromosomes visible as condensed X-shaped forms. Label: "23 pairs of chromosomes (46 total)"

**Stage 4 (far right):** Zoom into one chromosome — it unwinds, revealing the familiar double helix from the previous page. The DNA strand extends off the edge of the panel, impossibly long. Label: "Each chromosome is one very long DNA molecule"

Helix rides along the zoom sequence like a tour guide, getting smaller as we zoom in, waving from different stages.
