## Goal of This Page

Introduce the Central Dogma (DNA → RNA → Protein) and teach TRANSCRIPTION — the first half of the process. Just two concepts: (1) information flows one way: DNA → RNA → Protein, and (2) transcription is the process of copying DNA into messenger RNA. The reader should understand that DNA is the master blueprint that never leaves the vault, and mRNA is the working copy sent to the factory floor.

Translation (RNA → Protein) is on the NEXT page. Splitting this across two pages gives each process room to land.

## Panel Layout

### Slots 1-2 (top-left, merged 2 slots) — "The Most Important Idea"

Wide panel. Helix stands at a chalkboard (cream-colored, fits the teaching page aesthetic). Written on the board in bold, clean lettering:

**DNA → RNA → PROTEIN**

Below it, in Helix's handwriting: "The Central Dogma of Molecular Biology"

Helix points at the arrow between DNA and RNA with a pointer stick. Speech bubble: "This is the most important idea in all of biology. Information flows in one direction. DNA makes RNA. RNA makes protein. Almost never backwards."

Nityesh sits in front of the chalkboard, taking it in. Speech bubble: "So DNA is like... a recipe book? And RNA is a photocopy of one recipe?"

Helix: "Close. Let me show you exactly how it works."

### Slot 3-4 (top-right, merged 2 slots) — "The Vault"

The FACTORY METAPHOR begins — a beautiful, detailed cross-section illustration:

A bank vault (representing the nucleus) with thick walls. Inside the vault: a massive, glowing master blueprint scroll (the DNA). The scroll is labeled "MASTER BLUEPRINT — DO NOT REMOVE."

A small worker enzyme character (RNA polymerase — drawn as a cute factory robot with goggles) sits at the blueprint, carefully copying a section onto a smaller piece of paper (the mRNA). The copy is labeled "WORKING COPY."

An arrow shows the working copy being carried through a vault door (nuclear pore) by a messenger character heading toward a sign that reads "FACTORY FLOOR →"

Handwritten annotations:
- Arrow to the vault: "NUCLEUS — the cell's secure vault"
- Arrow to the robot: "RNA POLYMERASE — the copying enzyme"
- Arrow to the working copy: "mRNA — messenger RNA"
- Small note: "RNA uses Uracil (U) instead of Thymine (T) — the only letter change"

### Slots 5-6 (bottom-left, merged 2 slots) — "Genes"

A zoomed view of the master blueprint (DNA). Most of it is grayed out. A small highlighted section glows — this is a GENE.

Helix points at it: "A gene is a specific section of DNA that contains the instructions to build one protein. You have about 20,000-25,000 genes."

Visual annotation: The highlighted gene section is shown being read by the RNA polymerase robot, one base at a time, building the mRNA strand. The template strand of DNA is labeled, and the growing mRNA strand shows complementary bases being added (with U replacing T).

Handwritten note: "The enzyme reads the DNA template and builds a complementary mRNA copy. Like taking a photograph of one page of the blueprint."

### Slot 7 (bottom-center-right, 1 slot) — Nityesh Gets It

Nityesh, nodding slowly, connecting the pieces.

Speech bubble: "So the cell never lets the original blueprint out of the vault? It always sends copies?"

Helix: "Too precious to risk. The DNA stays safe in the nucleus. Only copies leave."

### Slot 8 (bottom-right, 1 slot) — The Hook

The mRNA exits the vault through the nuclear pore. On the other side, we catch a glimpse of the "factory floor" — the cytoplasm, where a large machine (the ribosome) sits waiting, ready to receive the mRNA.

Helix, now outside the vault, beckoning Nityesh to follow: "Now watch what happens when the copy reaches the factory floor..."

The ribosome looms large and detailed in the background, inviting curiosity.
