## Goal of This Page

Introduce Gregor Mendel and the concept of alleles. Just TWO concepts: (1) Mendel was a monk who discovered inheritance laws by studying pea plants for 8 years, and (2) alleles are different versions of the same gene — you carry TWO copies of each gene, one from each parent. This page should feel like arriving in a beautiful garden with a wise, patient teacher.

## Panel Layout

### Slots 1-2 (top-left, merged 2 slots) — "The Garden"

Wide establishing panel. A warm, sunlit monastery garden — rows of pea plants in bloom, some with purple flowers, some with white. Stone walls of the monastery visible in the background. Golden afternoon light. Helix sits on a garden wall, legs dangling, looking at home in this setting.

Nityesh walks into the garden, looking around with curiosity.

Helix: "1866. An Augustinian friar named Gregor Mendel spent EIGHT YEARS cross-breeding pea plants in a garden just like this one. He counted every flower, every seed, every trait. And he discovered the fundamental laws of inheritance — ninety years before anyone knew DNA existed."

### Slot 3 (top-center-right, 1 slot) — "The Insight"

A portrait-style panel showing a sketch of Mendel — round glasses, kind face, holding a pea pod. Rendered in a warm, historical illustration style (sepia tones, like an old journal drawing).

Handwritten annotation below: "He published his findings. Nobody noticed. He died unknown. His work was rediscovered in 1900."

### Slot 4 (top-right, 1 slot) — Nityesh Reacts

Nityesh, looking at the Mendel portrait with genuine respect.

Speech bubble: "Eight years of counting pea plants. And nobody cared?"

Helix: "Not until decades later. But what he found changed everything."

### Slots 5-6 (bottom-left, merged 2 slots) — "Alleles"

The key concept panel. Helix holds up two cards in front of a pea plant:

Card 1: **P** (purple, in a bright purple color)
Card 2: **p** (white, in a light gray/white color)

Helix: "Here's what Mendel figured out. Every trait is controlled by a gene. But genes come in different VERSIONS — called alleles. This plant's flower-color gene has two possible alleles: P for purple, p for white."

Below, a simple diagram showing a pea plant with two slots labeled "Copy 1" and "Copy 2" — each slot can hold either P or p.

Handwritten annotation: "You have TWO copies of each gene — one from mom, one from dad. That's your hand."

### Slot 7 (bottom-center-right, 1 slot) — Nityesh Gets It

Nityesh looking at his own hands, making the connection to himself.

Speech bubble: "So I'm walking around with TWO versions of every gene? And they might be different from each other?"

### Slot 8 (bottom-right, 1 slot) — The Hook

Helix holds up a card combination: one P and one p together. A question mark floats above the combination.

Helix: "Exactly. Now — what happens when they ARE different? If you have one purple allele and one white allele... what color is the flower?"

Nityesh: "I'd guess... purple? Or maybe a mix?"

Helix grins: "Let's find out."
