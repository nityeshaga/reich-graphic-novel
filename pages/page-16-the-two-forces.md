## Goal of This Page

Natural selection and the sickle cell example. TWO concepts: (1) Natural selection — mutations that help survival spread, harmful ones are removed, most are neutral, and (2) Heterozygote advantage (sickle cell) — the same allele can be deadly or lifesaving depending on copies and context.

## Panel Layout

### Slots 1-2 (top-left, merged 2 slots) — "Force One: Selection"

Wide panel. A visual metaphor: natural selection as a SIEVE or FILTER. Mutations (represented as colored marbles) falling through a mesh:

- **Green marbles** (beneficial mutations): pass through easily, labeled "Helpful — spreads through the population"
- **Red marbles** (harmful mutations): caught by the sieve, labeled "Harmful — removed over generations"  
- **Gray marbles** (neutral mutations): drift through slowly, labeled "Neutral — no effect, just accumulates"

Helix: "Natural selection is the first force. If a mutation helps you survive and reproduce, it spreads. If it hurts, it's removed. Most mutations are neutral — they just drift along, doing nothing."

### Slot 3-4 (top-right, merged 2 slots) — "The Sickle Cell Story"

The most important example in all of genetics education. A split panel:

**Left half — "One Copy: Lifesaver":**
A person labeled "AS" (heterozygous). Their red blood cells are normal-shaped. A malaria parasite tries to enter but is blocked. Label: "Heterozygous (AS) = resistant to malaria"

**Right half — "Two Copies: Deadly":**
A person labeled "SS" (homozygous). Their red blood cells are sickle-shaped, clogging a blood vessel. Label: "Homozygous (SS) = sickle cell disease"

Between the two halves: a map of Africa with malaria zones highlighted. In these zones, the AS genotype is strongly selected FOR — keeping the S allele common.

Helix: "The SAME allele. One copy saves your life. Two copies can kill you. In malaria country, carrying one sickle cell allele is an advantage."

### Slot 5 (bottom-left, 1 slot) — Nityesh Reacts

Nityesh, genuinely struck by this.

Speech bubble: "So the same mutation can be deadly or lifesaving depending on whether you have one copy or two? AND depending on where you live?"

### Slot 6 (bottom-center-left, 1 slot) — Helix Confirms

Helix: "Context is everything. In Norway, carrying sickle cell is just a risk. In West Africa, it's a survival advantage. Selection doesn't have opinions — it just responds to the environment."

### Slot 7 (bottom-center-right, 1 slot) — The Pivot

Helix holds up a hand — "but wait" gesture.

Speech bubble: "But selection is LOGICAL — it acts on fitness. The second force is the opposite. It's pure randomness. And in small populations, it can be MORE powerful than selection."

### Slot 8 (bottom-right, 1 slot) — The Hook

Nityesh: "Randomness? How can randomness be powerful?"

Helix holds up a coin: "Flip this ten times. How close to 50/50 do you get?"

Nityesh: "Probably not that close."

Helix: "Exactly. Now imagine your population IS those ten coin flips."
