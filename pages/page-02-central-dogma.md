# Chapter 1, Page 2: From Code to Life (The Central Dogma)

IMPORTANT: Do NOT number the panels.

## Characters

**Nityesh:** A pixel-art style young Indian man with distinctive big curly black hair, rectangular glasses, light stubble, white t-shirt, and beaded necklace. Pixel-art aesthetic — visible chunky pixels, retro game feel.

**Helix:** A small, friendly, anthropomorphic double-helix DNA strand. Coral/salmon pink strands with teal/cyan rungs (base pairs). Expressive cartoon eyes near the top, small stubby arms and legs. Standard comic style (NOT pixel-art).

## Goal of This Page

Teach the Central Dogma (DNA → RNA → Protein) with enough depth to understand transcription, translation, codons, and why proteins matter. The reader should understand HOW genetic information becomes a living organism. This is the "how does the cookbook actually get cooked?" page.

## Panel Layout

### Slots 1-3 (top row, merged 3 slots) — "The Central Dogma: DNA → RNA → Protein"

A WIDE panoramic panel showing the entire central dogma as a beautiful factory/production line metaphor:

**LEFT SECTION — THE VAULT (Nucleus):**
A grand vault door labeled "NUCLEUS." Inside, the DNA double helix is shown as a master blueprint stored on a scroll, glowing faintly. It's precious — it never leaves the vault.

An enzyme character labeled "RNA Polymerase" (depicted as a small machine/robot) opens the DNA strands, reads one strand, and assembles a messenger RNA (mRNA) copy. The mRNA is shown as a single-stranded ribbon being built letter by letter.

Above this section, bold label: "TRANSCRIPTION"
Annotation: "The master blueprint never leaves the vault. Instead, a working copy (mRNA) is made and sent to the factory floor."
Small note: "RNA uses Uracil (U) instead of Thymine (T) — the only difference from DNA."

**CENTER SECTION — THE MESSENGER:**
The mRNA ribbon travels through a pore in the vault/nucleus wall, heading toward the factory floor. It's a single strand, carrying the copied instructions.

**RIGHT SECTION — THE FACTORY (Ribosome):**
A ribosome (depicted as a large chunky machine with two halves clamped around the mRNA) reads the mRNA three letters at a time. Small transfer RNA (tRNA) molecules — shown as tiny L-shaped delivery trucks — arrive carrying amino acids (shown as colored beads). Each tRNA has a three-letter code on its "license plate" that matches a three-letter section of the mRNA.

The amino acids click together into a growing chain — a polypeptide — shown emerging from the ribosome like a string of colored beads.

Above this section, bold label: "TRANSLATION"
Annotation: "The ribosome reads the mRNA like a ticker tape — three letters at a time. Each triplet (codon) = one amino acid."

At the far right, the polypeptide chain folds into a 3D protein shape (shown as a blob with a specific shape, like a lock-and-key enzyme).

### Slot 4 (top-right, 1 slot) — "The Genetic Code"

A compact, colorful reference table showing examples of codons → amino acids:

AUG → Methionine (START — every protein begins here)
UUU → Phenylalanine
GCA → Alanine
UAA → STOP

Bold text: "64 possible codons → 20 amino acids + 3 stop signals"

Annotation: "The code has redundancy — multiple codons can code for the same amino acid. That's why some mutations are 'silent.'"

Below: "This code is nearly UNIVERSAL across all life — bacteria, plants, humans. Evidence of common ancestry billions of years ago."

Helix, pointing at the table: "Four letters. Three at a time. Twenty amino acids. That's the entire translation dictionary."

### Slots 5-6 (bottom-left, merged 2 slots) — "What Proteins Do"

A vibrant collage showing the incredible diversity of what proteins do — six mini-illustrations arranged in a grid:

1. **Enzymes** — a lock-and-key illustration showing an enzyme catalyzing a chemical reaction. Label: "Speed up reactions (enzymes)"
2. **Structure** — a collagen fiber or keratin strand. Label: "Build structures (collagen, keratin)"
3. **Transport** — a hemoglobin molecule carrying oxygen. Label: "Carry things (hemoglobin)"
4. **Defense** — an antibody (Y-shaped) attacking a virus. Label: "Fight invaders (antibodies)"
5. **Signaling** — insulin molecule and a cell receptor. Label: "Send messages (hormones)"
6. **Movement** — muscle fibers contracting. Label: "Create movement (actin, myosin)"

Bold text below the grid: "Proteins do EVERYTHING. A gene is ultimately an instruction to build a specific protein."

### Slot 7 (bottom-center-right, 1 slot) — "Wait, THAT Protein?" + The 1.5% Surprise

This panel does double duty — a personal connection moment AND a key fact.

**TOP HALF — The Protein Shake Moment:**
Nityesh holding a protein shake shaker bottle, "aha moment" expression — eyes wide, pointing at the shake. Helix stands on the rim of the shaker.

Nityesh: "Wait — the protein in my morning shake is the SAME protein?"

Helix: "Exactly! Whey, chicken, lentils — all proteins made from amino acid chains. Your body breaks them down, then YOUR ribosomes rebuild them into YOUR proteins using YOUR DNA recipes."

A small arrow diagram: Protein shake → amino acids → reassembled by YOUR ribosomes → YOUR proteins

Annotation: "You're not absorbing someone else's proteins. You're recycling amino acid building blocks and following your OWN instruction manual."

**BOTTOM HALF — The 1.5% Surprise:**
A small pie chart showing only ~1.5% of the human genome codes for proteins. The vast majority (98.5%) shown in light gray — was once called "junk DNA."

Annotation: "Now we know much of the 'junk' has regulatory functions — controlling WHEN and WHERE genes are turned on. We'll come back to this when we get to epigenetics."

Helix: "Your genome is 98.5% stage directions and 1.5% actual lines. The directing matters as much as the script."

### Slot 8 (bottom-right, 1 slot) — Hook Panel

Nityesh, looking thoughtful:

"Okay, so DNA makes RNA, RNA makes protein, proteins build everything. But how do I get MY specific DNA? Why do I look like my parents?"

Helix, with a grin: "That's where a monk with some pea plants changed everything. 1866."

## Visual Design Notes

- WARM CREAM/CHALKBOARD BACKGROUND — consistent with all Helix teaching pages
- The factory metaphor in slots 1-3 is the CENTERPIECE — it should be gorgeous, detailed, and immediately readable. The flow from left (vault/nucleus) to right (factory/ribosome) should feel like a journey.
- Color consistency with Page 1: A=green, T=red, G=blue, C=yellow. Add U=orange for RNA.
- The protein collage in slots 5-6 should feel vibrant and diverse — showing that proteins aren't one thing, they're EVERYTHING.
- Helix is animated and enthusiastic throughout — pointing at diagrams, explaining with gestures
- The factory metaphor should feel TANGIBLE: vault = nucleus, working copy = mRNA, factory = ribosome, delivery trucks = tRNA, product = protein. Each element is labeled both with its metaphor name and its biology name.
