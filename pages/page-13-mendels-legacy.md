# Chapter 2, Page 7: Mendel's Legacy

A landscape-format educational comic book page (2048x1440px). This is a HELIX TEACHING PAGE — warm cream/chalkboard background tone, freeform layout that feels like a whiteboard session. This page is a BREATHING PAGE — lighter, reflective, connecting what was learned back to the bigger journey.

The style is polished, modern comic book — clean lines, vibrant colors, professional typography mixed with handwritten-style annotations.

IMPORTANT: Do NOT number the panels. No circled numbers or digits in panel corners.

## Characters

**Nityesh:** A pixel-art style young Indian man with distinctive big curly black hair, rectangular glasses, light stubble, white t-shirt, and beaded necklace. Expressive cartoon eyes. The curly hair is his most defining visual feature — big, voluminous, immediately recognizable. Pixel-art aesthetic — visible chunky pixels, retro game feel, but clearly this specific person.

**Helix:** A small, friendly, anthropomorphic double-helix DNA strand. Coral/salmon pink strands with teal/cyan rungs (base pairs) connecting them. Expressive cartoon eyes near the top, small stubby arms and legs. Approachable, curious, slightly nerdy. Standard comic style (NOT pixel-art). About 1/4 of Nityesh's height — a small companion character.

## Goal of This Page

A synthesis/breathing page that consolidates Chapter 2's learning and bridges to Chapter 3 (mutations). TWO things to land: (1) A visual summary of the journey from Mendel's clean single-gene world to the reality of polygenic traits and allele frequencies — showing the reader how much ground they've covered, and (2) the natural question that launches Chapter 3: if alleles come in different versions, how are new versions created?

This page should feel like pausing on a hilltop to look back at how far you've climbed before tackling the next section.

## Panel Layout

### Slots 1-3 (top row, merged 3 slots) — "The Road You've Traveled"

Wide cinematic panel. A PATHWAY flowing left to right across the panel, like a trail through a landscape. Along the path, four landmarks — each one representing a stage of what Nityesh has learned in Chapter 2:

**Landmark 1:** A small monastery garden with pea plants — purple and white flowers, PP/Pp/pp written on signs. A garden gate marks the entrance. Label: "Mendel's garden — one gene, two outcomes"

**Landmark 2:** A fork in the path where colors blend — pink flowers (incomplete dominance), blood type tiles (codominance). Label: "Shared power — alleles that don't follow Mendel's simple rules"

**Landmark 3:** A music studio with a massive mixing board visible through the window, hundreds of sliders glowing. Label: "The symphony — thousands of genes, each a tiny nudge"

**Landmark 4:** Two population circles at the end of the trail, glowing with their characteristic dot patterns (different allele frequencies). A magnifying glass hovers over them. Label: "The fingerprint — allele frequencies, the tool Reich uses"

Helix walks the path like a trail guide, looking back over his shoulder: "Look how far you've come. You started with one monk counting peas. Now you understand that traits are shaped by thousands of genes, and that comparing the FREQUENCY of alleles across populations is how scientists read human history."

### Slot 4 (top-right, 1 slot) — Nityesh Reflects

Nityesh, sitting on a stone at the end of the trail, relaxed — the posture of someone who's absorbed something real. He looks back at the path behind him.

Speech bubble: "It all comes back to alleles. Different versions of the same gene. How common each version is in a population. That's what Reich measures."

### Slots 5-6 (bottom-left, merged 2 slots) — "The Quiet Realization"

Nityesh, still sitting on the stone, looking at the two population circles at the end of the trail. A thought forms slowly — not rushed, but inevitable.

Speech bubble: "So allele frequencies are different between populations. And those differences are what Reich reads. But... that means at some point, all those different allele versions had to be CREATED. There was a time when only one version existed. Then a second one appeared."

He looks at Helix with genuine curiosity.

Nityesh: "Where do new alleles come from?"

Helix pauses. A beat of silence. Then a small, serious smile.

Helix: "From mistakes."

### Slot 7 (bottom-center-right, 1 slot) — "Mistakes?"

Nityesh, surprised. This wasn't what he expected.

Speech bubble: "Mistakes? What kind of mistakes?"

Helix: "When a cell copies its 3.2 billion letters of DNA, sometimes it gets one wrong. One wrong letter out of billions. A MUTATION. And that one mistake creates a new allele that never existed before."

Visual: A tiny DNA strand with one letter glowing red — the wrong letter. Simple, evocative, not a full diagram.

### Slot 8 (bottom-right, 1 slot) — The Bridge

Nityesh stands up from the stone, energized, leaning forward.

Speech bubble: "Show me. How do these mistakes work? What happens to them?"

Helix hops forward, leading the way off the hilltop trail. The warm, garden-path tones of the page begin to shift — cooler blues and purples creeping in at the edges, hinting at the molecular world of Chapter 3.

Helix: "Follow me. This is where biology becomes history."
