The suggested one-line explanation ("because females have two X chromosomes and males have one, any given X chromosome is twice as likely to be in a female body") should be added.

## 4. TEACHING CLARITY

### Does the split work?

**Yes.** The split is effective for three reasons:

1. **Page 03 now teaches exactly ONE idea:** dominant vs recessive inheritance. Alleles, Punnett squares, trait-skipping — all in service of that single concept. The Mendel garden opener provides emotional grounding. The vocabulary panel provides reference. The three-generation family story provides the "aha." This page has real room to breathe.

2. **Page 03B escalates cleanly:** it opens with "Mendel was the simple case" and walks through three complications (codominance, polygenic, sex-linked), each with its own panel. The progression from "one gene, two alleles" to "hundreds of genes, population-level patterns" is a genuine conceptual ramp.

3. **The bridge to Reich lands properly.** In the original combined page, the polygenic/population-frequency insight was crammed alongside five other concepts. Now it gets a full 2-slot panel (slots 5-6) with a dedicated callout box. This is THE connection point between "genetics basics" and "the rest of the book." It deserves the space.

### Pacing

Page 03 feels relaxed — almost leisurely. This is correct. The reader just processed the Central Dogma on page 02; they need a gentler pace before complexity ramps up again.

Page 03B is denser — five concepts across 8 slots (codominance, incomplete dominance, Mendel's laws, polygenic traits, sex-linked). This is more packed than page 03, but each concept gets its own panel, which is a huge improvement over the original single-page version. The only risk is that slot 3 (incomplete dominance) and slot 4 (Mendel's laws) feel like quick sidebars compared to the meatier surrounding panels.

### Science notes

- **Page 03, Slots 5-7:** Eye color is used as the dominant/recessive example (brown B, blue b). The batch-1 review noted that eye color is actually polygenic. Page 03B now explicitly teaches polygenic traits, so the simplified eye-color model on page 03 is defensible — but there's still no asterisk connecting them. Add a small note: "simplified — eye color involves multiple genes, as we'll see on the next page."

- **Page 03B, Slots 1-2:** The blood type notation uses I^A, I^B, and i. This is correct (ABO blood group uses the I locus). The codominance explanation is accurate — I^A and I^B are codominant to each other, while both are dominant over i.

- **Page 03B, Slot 3:** Incomplete dominance in snapdragons is the textbook example and is accurately presented. Good.

- **Page 03B, Slot 7:** The claim "Color blindness: ~8% of men, <1% of women" — accurate for red-green color blindness in populations of European descent. Acceptable simplification.
